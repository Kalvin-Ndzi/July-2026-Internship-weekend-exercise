# Diagnostic Quiz

10 short questions. Not meant to be scary — meant to surface gaps quickly, in under 10 minutes. Encourage one-sentence answers, not essays.

1. What's the difference between `<section>` and `<article>`? Give an example of when you'd use each.

2. Why does every `<img>` need an `alt` attribute, even on a purely decorative image?

3. A student writes three `<h1>` tags on one page to make three pieces of text look big. What's wrong with this, and what should they do instead?

4. What connects a `<label>` to its `<input>`? What breaks if that connection is missing?

5. When would you use `<ol>` instead of `<ul>`? Give a real example.

6. What's the difference between `<main>` and `<div class="main">`?

7. Why is `<table>` the wrong choice for laying out a whole page, even though it can technically make things line up in columns?

8. What does the `<nav>` tag do that a plain `<div>` full of links doesn't?

9. A form has a text input for entering an email address. What's the actual practical benefit of using `type="email"` instead of `type="text"`?

10. Why does heading order matter (h1 → h2 → h3, no skipping) even if you're going to make everything the same visual size with CSS later?

---

## Grading note

The goal isn't a perfect score — it's spotting patterns. If most of the class misses the same question, that's your signal for what to revisit before moving on, not a reason to mark everyone down.
